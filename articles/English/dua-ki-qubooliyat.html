The Times and Etiquette of Acceptance of Supplication

Supplication (dua) is the essence of worship, and the Hadith describe certain special times and manners which, when observed, increase the likelihood of a supplication being accepted.

Recommended Times of Acceptance

The last part of the night before dawn, the time between the call to prayer and its establishment, a special hour on Friday, and the state of prostration are considered special times for the acceptance of supplication.

The Etiquette of Supplication

Praising Allah before supplication, reciting Durood, raising both hands, and supplicating with conviction are part of proper etiquette. Humility and heartfelt attentiveness during supplication are equally important.

The Need for Patience

Sometimes the result of a supplication is not immediately apparent. Islamic teaching holds that Allah accepts every supplication in one form or another — either granting what was asked, removing some harm in its place, or storing it as a reward for the Hereafter.
