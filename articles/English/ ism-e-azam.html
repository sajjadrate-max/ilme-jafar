What Is Ism-e-Azam (The Greatest Name)?

"Ism-e-Azam" refers to those Beautiful Names of Allah that are traditionally believed to carry special spiritual significance. Islamic tradition holds a belief that when a person supplicates using the Ism-e-Azam, their prayer draws closer to acceptance.

The Traditional Method of Seeking It

In traditional Ilm-e-Jafar, the Abjad value of the questioner's name is used in an attempt to determine which of Allah's attributive Names might be especially suited for that individual to recite. This is a symbolic, traditional method — not a claim to definitive scientific fact.

The Importance of the Beautiful Names

The Qur'an mentions 99 Beautiful Names of Allah, each expressing a particular attribute of His. Memorizing, understanding, and supplicating through these Names is considered a highly commendable practice in Islamic teaching.

A Note of Caution

Scholars hold differing views on exactly which Name constitutes the "Ism-e-Azam," and no single Name has been definitively established from the Qur'an and Hadith. It is therefore best that a person supplicates sincerely through all of Allah's Beautiful Names.
