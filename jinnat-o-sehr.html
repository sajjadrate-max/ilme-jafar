The Islamic Perspective on Jinn and Sorcery

The Qur'an clearly mentions the existence of jinn, and an entire chapter, Surah Al-Jinn, is devoted to this subject. According to Islamic belief, jinn are a separate creation who, like humans, can be either righteous or wicked.

The Reality of Sorcery

Sorcery (sihr) is also mentioned in the Qur'an and Hadith, and Islam strictly forbids it as a major sin. Both performing sorcery and having it performed are prohibited.

Recommended Methods of Protection

Reciting Al-Mu'awwidhatayn, Ayat al-Kursi, the morning and evening remembrances, and frequent recitation of the Qur'an are considered authentic and recommended means of protection from the effects of jinn and sorcery.

A Word of Caution

If someone suspects the influence of sorcery or jinn, they should consult a trustworthy, pious, and knowledgeable scholar. One should strictly avoid going to any fraudulent or unqualified practitioner.
